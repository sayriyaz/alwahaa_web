Police Clearance Certificate in the UAE: what visitors changing to employment should know

A Police Clearance Certificate, also called a Good Conduct Certificate, confirms whether the applicant has a criminal record in the issuing country or emirate. In the UAE, it is commonly used for employment, immigration, licensing, professional registration and selected government procedures.

For some visitor-to-employee cases, employers or authorities may ask for PCC before the work permit, visa status change or residency file can move forward.

Why PCC is coming up in visitor-to-employee cases

Many UAE candidates enter on a visit visa, secure a job offer, then move into employment visa processing. In straightforward cases, the file may proceed with standard labour, immigration, medical and Emirates ID steps. But depending on the applicant's nationality, job role, employer policy, authority channel or risk check, a Police Clearance Certificate may be requested before the employment process is completed.

This does not mean every applicant needs PCC. It means the document should be checked early, especially where the applicant is converting from visit status to employment and the employer wants the file cleared quickly.

Who may be asked for it?

  • Visitors changing status to employment for selected nationalities or authority channels
  • Applicants for regulated, sensitive or trust-based roles
  • Candidates whose employer requests PCC as part of internal onboarding
  • Applicants whose previous UAE residence history needs local good-conduct confirmation
  • People asked to submit a home-country police certificate with UAE attestation or legal translation

UAE-issued PCC vs home-country PCC

A UAE Police Clearance Certificate is usually issued through the UAE police or Ministry of Interior channels and reflects the record held in the UAE. A home-country PCC is issued by the applicant's country of nationality or previous residence. Some cases need one, some need the other, and some may need both depending on the requesting authority.

Documents commonly requested

  • Clear passport copy
  • Current UAE visa, visit visa or previous residence details where available
  • Emirates ID copy if the applicant currently has, or previously had, UAE residency
  • Recent passport-size photograph
  • Fingerprint card or embassy/police verification where the applicant is outside the UAE
  • Legal translation and attestation if a foreign PCC is not accepted in its original format

How long does it take?

UAE-issued certificates are often faster when the applicant's details are clear and the required identity records are available. Foreign certificates can take longer because they may involve home-country police systems, embassy coordination, attestation, courier handling and legal translation.
